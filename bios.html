01 / BK

BK (he/él)

Co-Steward · Regenerative Technologist · Systems & Governance Coach

BK is a philosopher and social commentator focused on the paradigm-shift happening in how we work. As a skilled convenor and systems leader, he illuminates what lies beyond "business as usual" — a more relational and integral approach, in which self-study provides a means for greater purpose and meaning, and how we live is valued as much as the work we do.

He brings over fifteen years spanning private sector, cooperatives, nonprofits, and community organizing — moving between worlds most consultants occupy only one of. His background across a variety of organizations provides a strong vantage point to view the transition from extractive to regenerative models of work. He is a community builder, systems thinker, and organizational coach whose highest aim is to co-create conditions where people can flourish.

BK is a certified IC-Agile Coach and trained Sociocracy Consultant, grounded in consent-based governance and distributed decision-making. His systems thinking draws from cybernetics, Nora Bateson's warm data methodology, and the living systems tradition. He is a practitioner of Warm Data, designing and hosting labs that surface patterns and relationships conventional analysis misses.

He brings contemplative practice, Buddhist epistemology, and a monastic approach to work — not as philosophy, but as a way of paying attention that changes what becomes visible. His approach integrates trauma-informed leadership, nonviolent communication, and Dragon Dreaming into organizational contexts.

Outside of Limicelia, BK is building the Present of Work — a translocal ecosystem and podcast exploring how modern work, workers, and workplaces can become more humane and alive. Conversations with leading-edge thinkers illuminate the paradigm-shift already underway. He also performs as DJ MadMarti, using sonic technology for collective transformation. He is a Montessori alum and lives in Richmond, Virginia.

02 / CX

CX (she/her)

Co-Steward · Organizational Transformation Designer

CX has worn a few hats: academic researcher, startup co-founder, podcast host, and discovery partner for leaders and organizations on their paths of learning and transformation. Her purpose has always remained the same: to help unleash human potential through a deeper understanding of people, work, and community.

She holds a Ph.D. in Management Science and Engineering from Stanford — and has spent her career translating that rigorous training into something warmer, more embodied, and more alive. Where most people with her credentials build models, she builds containers: experiential spaces that integrate contemplative, creative, and strategic elements into genuine learning and healing.

Her practice is rooted in a conviction that organizational transformation is not just a cognitive event. It is somatic — held in the body. It is relational — shaped by the quality of connection between people. And it is imaginative — requiring the capacity to see beyond what currently exists. Her work integrates complexity leadership, T-group and effective communication, psychological safety in teams, and trauma-informed collaboration.

CX designs experiential programs for organizational leaders, community stewards, and purpose-driven individuals across sectors. She brings particular depth to transformative learning experiences — retreats, intensives, and ongoing containers where leaders can do the inner work that their organizational roles demand but rarely make space for.

Based in the San Francisco Bay Area, she loves going on hikes with people from all walks of life — in-person, virtual, or metaphorical. She believes the best conversations happen in motion.

03 / Together

Co-stewards,
not executives.

BK and CX bring complementary orientations to the same work. BK reads organizations through governance, systems architecture, and the structural conditions that produce behavior. CX reads them through relational fields, somatic intelligence, and the experiential conditions that produce change. Together, they hold both the structural and the human — which is where transformation actually lives.

Their highest aim is to co-create a world where people can flourish — in their work, their communities, and themselves. They believe consulting can be different, more alive, when it returns to how systems in nature learn, adapt, and create space for lasting change.

Organizational home

Limicelia is a project of Inquiring Systems Institute (ISI), a 501(c)(3) fiscal sponsor. Organized as a practitioner collective — co-stewarded, not managed — with a commons pool model where a portion of every engagement funds access for solidarity economy organizations.

EP

EP

Executive Coach · Human-Centric Leadership · Team Culture

EP spent twenty years leading strategy and partnership teams at large global organizations — across countries as diverse as Brazil, Russia, India, China, and throughout Europe. She observed the same patterns of leadership and team dysfunction in every setting. Different languages, same breakdowns.

A serious accident during a business trip changed her trajectory. After months of recovery, the question shifted from what's good for my career? to what's the best way to use my life force? She left her position, completed her coaching education, and started over.

Working with teams steeped in tension, bully behavior, silent conflict, and false harmony, she noticed the root: people were seeing each other as titles and job functions, not whole humans. To address this, she developed Human-Centric Leading — a leadership model, mindset, and toolset that puts people at the center of decisions.

She holds an MBA, is a Professional Certified Coach (PCC) recognized by the International Coaching Federation, and is affiliated with UVA Darden Executive Education. She has published five Human-Centric Leading Toolkits and is the author of Reclaiming Our Humanity in the Workplace.

She co-leads Leaders for Good, a leadership development and team culture design firm. The differentiator is working at the mindset level before launching into structural transformation — and that changes everything.

NW

NW

Systems Leadership · Adult Development · Network Weaving

NW spent a decade as an operations leader — strategy, analytics, marketing, call center operations — holding eleven different roles in different parts of the same business. That range taught him something most specialists miss: how culture, communication, incentives, and technology affect teams in patterns that are real but invisible to the people inside them.

He moved into systems leadership, applying that understanding to complex challenges: improving customer experience for millions, supporting global crisis response, spreading a network mindset among NGOs, and weaving learning communities in Toronto.

He is a consultant at Cultivating Leadership, working with executives in tech, pharma, and consumer goods. He co-authored Human Work: Five Leadership Mindsets for Humanising the Workplace. He founded ThinkBetter, focused on systems mapping and collective sense-making.

Recently he led a team mapping the Wellbeing Economies Landscape across Canada — roughly 500 organizations, projects, and people working toward an economy that serves people and planet first. He is COO at Sareta, an AI materials science startup, where he is responsible for growing a healthy organization from the inside out.

He is based in Toronto. Outside of work, he DJs, canoes, camps, and is learning what 'villaging' looks like as a new parent.

IC

IC

Systems Design · Emotional Complexity · Nervous System Theory

IC is a systems designer and strategist. Her focus is specific and unusual: helping designers and teams engage with the emotional complexity surrounding problems — so they stop perpetuating systems of oppression in their work and in their team dynamics.

She uses distress as a tool for design. Where most practitioners try to manage tension out of the room, she works with the despair, confusion, and insecurity that complex systemic injustice actually produces — treating emotional unrest as a catalyst for reimagining solutions at the margins of collective imagination.

Her approach integrates design thinking, nervous system theory, and complexity science. With over a decade of consulting experience in reproductive justice, ed-tech justice, liberation movements, and maternal health, she recently led a three-year grant initiative focused on reducing infant mortality in Brooklyn.

She writes about recurring conflict in high-performing teams — how implicit memory powers the 'elevator ride' from observation to certainty, and how teams can learn to slow down the meaning-making process enough to actually see what's happening. She holds a BS in Global Studies from The New School.

She was Designer in Residence at SVA's Design for Social Innovation program. Her collaborations include the NAACP Climate Justice Working Group, ACLU of Northern California, and Elephant Circle.

Her favorite social spaces: holiday dinners, barbershops, packed kitchens, rap battles, and workshops of unlikely collaborators.
