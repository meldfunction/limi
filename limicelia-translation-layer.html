Key concepts — translation pairs

AI strategy that answers "why" before selecting tools

AI North Star

Values-based framework: why are we using AI beyond productivity? What won't we do? How do we measure regenerative vs. extractive impact? Comes before tool selection — not after.

Using AI to do more meaningful work instead of just more work

Organizational Imagination

The practice of asking: what deep work has never had time? What quality was sacrificed for speed? Without this, AI simply intensifies existing patterns — more of what was already burning people out.

AI making work harder even as it makes tasks easier

Workload Creep

UC Berkeley research: AI makes tasks easier, so workers do more, expectations rise to match new capacity, cognitive load exceeds productivity gains. The structural cause of AI-era burnout.

Practitioner network — quality assured through relationship, not credentials or hierarchy

The Liminal Web

Mycelium logic, not org chart. Distributed, resilient, no single center. Practitioners enter through Starter Cultures and Intentional Society — trust built through showing up, not applications.

The meaningful work that happens in transitions, between old forms and new ones

Liminal space

Where old forms are composting and new ones haven't yet solidified. Where most important organizational work actually happens — and where most organizations have no equipped partner.

Organizations as living systems — asking what the system wants to do, not who is broken

Symmathesy (Nora Bateson)

Mutual learning in living systems. The diagnostic question is never "who is broken" but "what does this system want to do?" Structure produces behavior — changing behavior without changing structure is rearranging furniture in a burning building.

Open-source methodology — frameworks and tools returned to the field, not held as proprietary IP
