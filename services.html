Custom Journeys
01

We don't offer
a service menu.

We build custom journeys. The domains below are starting shapes — not a menu to order from. Most engagements draw from several simultaneously, and all share the same underlying arc: listen from the frontline up, name what the system produces, stay through the messy middle, leave capacity not a report.

If you know something is broken but you're not sure what it needs — that is exactly where we start. The diagnostic conversation is not a sales call. It is genuine inquiry into what the situation is asking for.

Seven Domains
02

The seven
service domains.

Organizational Design & Governance
Sociocracy · Consent-Based Decision-Making · Role Clarity
Sociocratic and participatory structures, decision-making frameworks, role clarity and accountability systems that reflect how the organization actually wants to operate — not how the org chart says it should. For nonprofits in governance crisis, foundations rethinking board structure, co-ops scaling.
6–9 months
Leadership & Culture Transformation
Inner-Outer Integration · Culture Change · Relational Repair
Accompanying leaders and teams through inner and outer work simultaneously — shifting culture, healing relational dynamics, building conditions for sustained change without burning out the people doing it. For burned-out leadership teams, organizations in cultural crisis, post-scandal recovery.
6–9 months
Community Engagement & Participatory Design
Frontline Intelligence · Co-Design · Not Consultation Theater
Bringing community intelligence into the center of strategy, service, and product — not as consultation theater but as actual co-design where what the community knows shapes what gets built. For foundations redesigning grantmaking, nonprofits serving communities, public agencies.
4–6 months
Conflict Resolution & Coalition Building
Deep Democracy (minority voices heard, not managed) · Power-Aware · Multi-Stakeholder
Facilitated processes for navigating conflict, cross-sector tension, and coalition formation in civic, cooperative, and multi-stakeholder environments where power differentials are real and need to be named, not managed.
3–6 months
Field Building & Ecosystem Convening
Warm Data Labs (structured conversations surfacing what standard analysis misses) · Open Space · Systems Convening
Designing and hosting convenings that strengthen connective tissue between practitioners, funders, researchers, and institutions at field edges. Not networking events — encounters that change how people think. For foundations convening grantees, ecosystem builders, networks in formation.
Per convening or ongoing engagement
Organizational Memory & Knowledge Systems
Harvest · Transition Accompaniment · Living Documents
Capturing, stewarding, and activating institutional knowledge — especially in leadership transitions and organizational crises where what was learned must not leave with the person who's leaving.
3–4 months
Technology & AI Strategy
North Star · Organizational Imagination · Regenerative Adoption
Community-led product development, regenerative AI adoption, knowledge management, data sovereignty. AI transformation that creates capacity for meaningful work rather than workload creep and burnout.

Engagement Types
03

Three ways
to enter.

Diagnostic · 3 months
What's actually broken
Discovery + deep listening + honest diagnosis. Deliverable: diagnostic report with recommended pathways. For organizations who know something's wrong but aren't sure what it needs.
Transformation · 6 months
Changing how you work
Full 6-phase arc: discovery through harvest. Redesigned structures, practices, and capacity that the organization owns after we leave.
Anchor Partner · 12+ months
Sustained accompaniment
Embedded practitioner through multiple transitions. Lasting organizational capacity and a practitioner relationship. For organizations navigating continuous transformation, meta-organizations, complex systems that need a sustained thinking partner.
Most engagements are custom — built at the intersection of several domains. We start with a diagnostic conversation, not a service selection. If you know what's broken but not what it needs, that's the right place to begin.
