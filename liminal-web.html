What It Is
01

Not a roster.
A web of relationships.

The Liminal Web is the connective tissue of Limicelia's work — a living network of practitioners who work at the edges — between disciplines, between sectors, between what is and what wants to emerge.

Liminal: because most important work happens in the between — in transitions, threshold moments, the space where old forms are composting and new ones haven't yet solidified. Web: because mycelial logic governs here — distributed, resilient, no single center, nutrients flowing where needed, strength emerging from connection not hierarchy.

Not a contractor pool. Not a vendor list. Not a certification program. When you work with Limicelia, you enter this web of relationships — practitioners who share practice, accountability, and the patient work of organizational restoration. Trust is built inside the web before any client engagement happens. That's not gatekeeping. It's how quality gets protected without hierarchy.

For Clients
02

What this means
when you work with us.

When you engage Limicelia, you don't get a solo consultant. You enter a web of practitioners who share practice, mutual accountability, and the patient work of organizational restoration. Trust is built inside the web before any client engagement happens.

Your practitioner is held by a network — supported by peer supervision, shared methodology, and a development ecosystem that cultivates presence, relational craft, and organizational skill. This means the quality of accompaniment you receive is not dependent on any single person's capacity on any given day.

Practitioners grow through practice, not programs — from contemplative ground and relational craft through organizational methods to integration into the Liminal Web itself. The progression is not certification. It is accumulating the actual capacities that hard organizational work demands.

Ecosystem
03

The web within
the wider field.

The Liminal Web doesn't exist in isolation. It sits inside a network of organizations that share aligned practice and values — each playing a different role in the ecosystem Limicelia is trying to help grow.

Present of Work — Field context and values. A translocal network and space of inquiry for post-growth alternatives to extractive work systems. No entity; pure field.

Starter Cultures — Inner cultivation home. Free practice circles. The ground-level community where Tier A and B development happens and where practitioners become known through practice.

Intentional Society — Relational craft and adult development. The container for T-Group, Circling, and interpersonal range work at Tier B.

CrowdWork DC — Practitioner pipeline and dual-use platform. Bridges conventional and cooperative work contexts.

Inquiring Systems Institute — Fiscal sponsor and 501(c)(3) structure. Provides legal, accounting, HR, and administrative infrastructure while keeping Limicelia mission-accountable and grant-eligible.
